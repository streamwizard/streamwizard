The clips page is where your highlights stop being a wall of thumbnails. Filter
them down, sort them how you think, and drop them into folders. This is the part
of StreamWizard you'll actually live in.

  <img src="/images/clips/clips-overview.png" alt="Clips page overview" />
</Frame>

## Grid or details

Top right of the page, a toggle switches between two layouts:

- **Grid:** thumbnail cards. Good for skimming visually.
- **Details:** a denser, row-based list. Good when you're scanning titles,
  views, and dates.

Your choice rides along in the URL, so a bookmarked or shared link keeps the
same view.

## Filtering your clips

The filter bar sits above your clips. Every control updates the list the moment
you change it. There's no **Apply** button. The filters live in the URL too, so
any filtered view is shareable and bookmarkable.

### Quick filters

Two filters sit out in the open:

- **Search:** free-text match on the clip title. Start typing and results
  update after a short pause; you need at least 3 characters before it kicks in.
- **Category:** narrow to a single Twitch category (the game or section the
  clip was made in).

### Advanced filters

Hit **Filters** to expand the rest. A badge on the button shows how many filters
are currently active.

<Frame caption="The advanced filters panel: streamer, clipped by, date range, featured, and sorting.">
  <img src="/images/clips/filters-panel.png" alt="Advanced filters panel" />
</Frame>

| Filter | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Streamer** | Show clips from a specific broadcaster's channel. |
| **Clipped by** | Show clips created by a specific person. |
| **Date range** | Limit to clips made in a window. Presets or a custom range. |
| **Featured only** | Show only clips marked featured on Twitch. |
| **Sort by** | Order by **Date** or **Views**. |
| **Order** | **Ascending** or **Descending**. Default is newest first. |

### Active filters and clearing them

When filters are on, a row of chips appears above the bar, one per active
filter. Click the **✕** on a chip to drop just that filter, or **Clear all** to
reset everything at once.

### Pulling in new clips

The **Sync** button next to the filters fetches your latest clips from Twitch on
demand. Handy if you didn't turn on auto-sync during
[onboarding](/onboarding), or you just finished a stream and don't want to wait.

## Folders

Folders are how you keep clips organized for real. They live in the sidebar
under **Clips → Folders**, and they nest. Folders can hold subfolders as deep as
you need.

<Frame caption="Folders live in the sidebar. Hover a row for its + and ⋮ actions.">
  <img src="/images/clips/folders-sidebar.png" alt="Folders in the sidebar" />
</Frame>

### Create a folder

- Click the **+** next to **Folders** in the sidebar to create a top-level
  folder.
- Name it (letters, numbers, spaces, hyphens, and underscores, up to 255
  characters) and confirm. Folder created. Slightly less chaotic now. 📁

### Create a subfolder

- Hover a folder row and click its **+** button, or open its **⋮** menu and
  choose **New subfolder**.
- The new folder is created inside the one you picked.

### Rename or delete a folder

Open a folder's **⋮** menu:

- **Rename:** give it a new name.
- **Delete:** removes the folder after a confirmation. Your clips aren't
  deleted. They just leave that folder and stay available everywhere else. If
  the folder has subfolders, those get deleted too.

### Browsing folders

Click any folder in the sidebar to see the clips inside it. A breadcrumb at the
top of the page shows where you are and lets you jump back up the tree. All the
filters and sorting above work inside a folder, too.

## Adding clips to folders

Every clip card has a **⋯** (more) button. Open it and you'll find a **Folders**
section:

<Frame caption="Add a clip to any folder. Subfolders open in a waterfall submenu.">
  <img src="/images/clips/add-to-folder.png" alt="Add to folder menu" />
</Frame>

- **Add to folder:** pick a folder from the list. Folders with subfolders open
  a nested submenu (a waterfall); the first entry of each submenu adds the clip
  to that parent folder directly, so you can drop it at any level. A clip can
  live in as many folders as you want, and folders it's already in are greyed
  out.
- **Remove from folder:** lists only the folders this clip is currently in.
  Pick one to pull the clip out of it. The clip itself stays in your library.

The same **⋯** menu also has quick actions: **View** on Twitch, **Download
Landscape**, **Download Portrait**, and **Copy URL to clipboard**.

## Empty folder?

If a folder has nothing in it yet, the page says so. Head back to all clips,
filter down to what you want, and add them with the **⋯** menu above.
